FAQ about HRDF

  • What is HRDF?

    The Human Resources Development Fund (HRDF) was established under the legal requirements of the Human Resources Development Act 1992 (currently known as the Pembangunan Sumber Manusia Berhad Act 2001). The HRDF is a pool of funds that consists of Human Resources Development levies collected from employers of the manufacturing and service sectors as listed in the First Schedule of the Pembangunan Sumber Manusia Berhad Act 2001 (liable registrants) as well as optional registrants.

    In a simpler way to say, the HRDF was established with the aim of developing quality human capital and world-class workforce in order for Malaysia to achieve a high income economy based on knowledge and innovation.

  • What is HRDL?

    HRDL is Human Resources Development Levy. According to Section 14(1) of PSMB Act, 2001 a HRD levy, mandatory levy payment imposed by the Government on specified groups of employers for the purpose of employee training and skills upgrading shall be paid by every employer to whom the PSMB Act, 2001 applies. Employers are liable to pay a Human Resource Development levy for each working employee at the rate of 1.0% of the monthly wages of the employee.

  • What are the benefits of HRDF?

    Training grants are open to all employers registered and/or incorporated in Malaysia who have registered with PSMB and pay the HRD levy as to defray all or a major portion of the costs incurred in employee training. Financial assistance is given to employers that conduct training that is in line with their business operations.

  • What is SBL?

    Skim Bantuan Latihan (SBL) is the main scheme under HRDF and its objective is to encourage employers to retrain and upgrade their employees’ skills in line with their operational and business requirements. Under SBL, employers are free to identify their own training needs and implement the training programs in stages.

    SBL requires employers to make full payment for the training programs and reimbursement is based on employer claims.

    Note: You are encouraged to apply for trainings that are in the SBL-Khas category. Please reach out to your Admin Assistant prior applying for any training.

  • What is SBL-Khas?

    Skim Bantuan Latihan (SBL) is one of the major schemes under HRDF. Financial assistance in the form of training grants may be considered for all types of training programs tailored to the needs of registered employers.

    The employer does not require paying the training fees to the training provider to reduce the employer’s burden. The course fee will be deducted from the employers’ account and paid directly to the training provider.

    Note: You are encouraged to apply for trainings that are in the SBL-Khas category. Please reach out to your Admin Assistant prior applying for any training.

  • What are the difference between SBL and SBL-Khas?

    SBL Scheme requires employers to make full payment for the training programs and reimbursement is based on employer claims. Under SBL, employers are free to identify their own training needs and implement the training programs in stages.

    The main difference between SBL Scheme and SBL-Khas Scheme is that employer does not pay the training provider directly under SBL-Khas Scheme, course fee will be deducted from the employers’ account and paid directly to the training provider.

    Though most training programs are HRDF claimable under SBL, only the training programs that are pre-approved by PSMB are HRDF claimable under SBL-Khas.

    Note: You are encouraged to apply for trainings that are in the SBL-Khas category. Please reach out to your Admin Assistant prior applying for any training.

  • What do I do if I need to apply for a training?

    If you are an Employee:

    Please reach out to your Supervisor/Staff Development Manager on the need to apply for a training. Include details such as justification and needs. Your Supervisor/Staff Development Manager will assess the training needs and proceed with the next steps.

    If you are a Supervisor/ Staff Development Manager:

    Assess the training needs for your employees. You may refer below the list of trainings available:

            2021 Training (Vendor: InfoTrek)
            2021 Training (Vendor: Trainocate)

    Note: Apart from the list above, you may use other ExxonMobil approved vendors.

    Please contact the vendor directly if you need more information about the training details that may suit your needs. There are two types of training categories: SBL and SBL-Khas. You are encouraged to apply for trainings that are in the SBL-Khas category. If the training that you need is in the SBL category, please contact the vendor on the requirements to change the category to SBL-Khas.

    Once the training has been identified, please contact your Admin Assistant to kick start the application process and include the following information:

            1.    Quotations from vendors (provide at least two for comparison)
                    a.    Course name and course code
                    b.    Dates
                    c.    Price
                    d.    Training category: SBL or SBL-Khas
                    e.    Vendor details
                    f.     Preferred training and vendor – include justification if the price is higher compared to the other
            2.    Attendee
            3.    Employment type
            4.    Cost center
            5.    Approval

    Note: Your Admin Assistant would be able to identify if there is still enough allocation from the fund to apply for the training or not.
